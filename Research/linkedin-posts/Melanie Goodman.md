## LinkedIn Algorithm Prioritizes Relevance Over Reach

- **Date (approx.)**: 2026-03-26 (shown as “1mo”)

I have scrapped all my posts scheduled for this week 🫨 Quite frankly, they bored me. They were beautiful, but all those infographics just looked like the last 10 posts in my feed.

Chasing trending topics will not build your LinkedIn presence.

I am well aware that everyone says "jump on trends, post about what's hot. Ride the wave..."

But the algorithm actually rewards semantic relevance to your audience, not momentary noise.

LinkedIn's feed now uses large language models to understand meaning. It matches your content to people based on what they genuinely care about, not what's trending on Twitter.

𝗪𝗵𝗲𝗻 𝘁𝗿𝗲𝗻𝗱-𝗰𝗵𝗮𝘀𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗯𝗮𝗰𝗸𝗳𝗶𝗿𝗲𝘀:
→ The topic has nothing to do with your expertise
→ Your audience scrolls past because it feels off-brand
→ You attract the wrong people who never come back
→ The algorithm learns you're inconsistent

𝗪𝗵𝗲𝗻 𝗶𝘁 𝗮𝗰𝘁𝘂𝗮𝗹𝗹𝘆 𝘄𝗼𝗿𝗸𝘀:
→ The trend intersects with your core expertise
→ You have a genuine perspective to add
→ Your existing audience would expect you to comment
→ It reinforces, rather than dilutes, your positioning

The partners and professionals I work with in law and finance don't need viral moments. They need consistent visibility with the right people over time.

One well-positioned post that reaches 500 of the right decision-makers will outperform a trending post that reaches 5,000 random scrollers.

The algorithm is getting smarter.
It rewards depth over breadth.
Relevance over reach.

Before you post about the latest headline, ask yourself: does this strengthen or scatter my positioning?

If you can't answer that clearly, the post isn't ready.

What's your filter for deciding whether to jump on a trend or let it pass?
